# Publication Policy

This repository contains frameworks, methodologies, and reflections from my professional practice as an educator and curriculum architect. Here is what is shared, what is anonymized, and why.

## What is published here

- **Frameworks and methodologies** I developed through my professional practice. These represent my intellectual contribution — diagnostic tools, curriculum design principles, and assessment philosophies that I created through observation, research, and iteration.
- **My personal AI workflow** — how I use AI tools in my daily practice. This is my own experience, documented with anonymized examples.
- **Aggregated, anonymized outcomes** — measurable results from my teaching, with no individually identifiable student data.

## What is anonymized

- **Institution name**: The school where I currently work is referred to generically (e.g., "a 4-campus IB international school"). This protects both the institution and my professional relationship with it.
- **Student data**: All student references are anonymized. No names, identifying details, or individual assessment scores appear in this repository. Aggregated results (e.g., "93% of students improved") are reported without individual attribution.
- **Colleague references**: No colleagues are named or individually identifiable.

## What is NOT published here

- **Institutional documents**: Internal proposals, strategic plans, and leadership communications created in my professional capacity remain confidential.
- **Individual student records**: No IEPs, progress reports, or individual assessments.
- **Proprietary school content**: Curricula owned by my employer, internal assessment tools, or school-specific operational documents.

## Maturity labels

Every artifact in this repository is labeled with its honest development stage:

| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| `observed` | A pattern I noticed across multiple contexts |
| `designed` | A framework I created to address the pattern |
| `proposed` | A framework I presented to institutional leadership |
| `piloted` | A framework tested with a small group |
| `validated` | A framework with measured outcomes and evidence |

These labels are a commitment to transparency. I do not present proposed work as validated, or designed work as shipped. The stage is part of the story.

## Why this matters

I believe in honest professional representation. The frameworks here represent real thinking applied to real problems. Some have been validated through measurable outcomes. Others are proposals awaiting implementation. Both have value — but they are different kinds of value, and I label them accordingly.
