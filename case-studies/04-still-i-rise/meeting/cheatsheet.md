# Still I Rise — Meeting Cheatsheet
## Dense paragraphs, not bullets. Read before the conversation.

## THE POSITIONING

You are a curriculum architect and a knowledge systems builder who have already designed AI-powered education systems for two schools — one IB international school in San Francisco (structural coherence, CEFR tracking, curriculum integration) and one adaptive learning platform (mastery-based progression, child profiles, coaching dashboards). You are not selling a product. You are proposing to adapt validated frameworks for a context you deeply respect. Lead with listening. Ask before presenting. Show what you've built only after you understand what they need. The one-liner: "We build AI systems that help teachers teach better — not by replacing them, but by giving them a governed knowledge system that tracks what works, suggests what's next, and never forgets what they've learned."

## THE 30-SECOND INTRO

"I'm Mical, a comparative linguist and knowledge architect. Claudia is a curriculum architect who designed the Italian language programme at a 4-campus IB school in San Francisco. Together we've built AI education systems for two schools — one focused on structural coherence across IB, language immersion, and Reggio, the other on adaptive learning with mastery-based progression for diverse learners. We want to understand if any of what we've built could serve your teachers and students."

## WHY THIS MATTERS TO US (be honest)

Claudia spent 6 years building curriculum for an IB school where three pedagogical traditions competed for limited time. The root cause was always the same: teachers doing extraordinary work without structural support. We imagine this is multiplied at Still I Rise — teachers working in extreme conditions with students who need even more. We're not here because we think AI solves refugee education. We're here because we think the frameworks we've built to reduce teacher burden and preserve institutional memory might help teachers who are already doing the hardest version of this work.

## WHAT WE KNOW ABOUT THEM (show you did the work)

They serve 76,000+ people across four countries. IB World School in Nairobi since 2024, opened Bogotá in 2024, opening Mumbai and Italy in 2026. Nobel Peace Prize nominated in 2023. 95%+ of donations go to programs. They refuse government funding. Their founder Nicolò Govoni built this from the ground up. They don't compromise on quality — same IB standard as any elite school, but free for refugee children. They call their students "the leaders of tomorrow," not beneficiaries. Respect this language.

## THE ITALY CONNECTION

Their Italy campus opens in 2026, serving migrant, Roma, and vulnerable Italian children. Claudia designed the only K-5 Italian language programme guide that integrates Indicazioni Ministeriali, CEFR, IB PYP, and Reggio into a single manual. This is directly relevant — offer it naturally, don't push it. If they ask about their Italy curriculum, Claudia is uniquely positioned to help design it.

## THREE THINGS TO SHOW IF ASKED

First, the CEFR framework. It works for any language pair. Their students acquiring English in Nairobi or Spanish in Bogotá need structured progression targets (A1→B1) with measurable milestones. We built this for Italian immersion. The structure transfers. Show the Lingua Viva manual structure — four sections per grade, vertical alignment, portfolio assessment.

Second, the 50/50 principle. Half structure, half flexibility. Teachers get non-negotiable minimums (what each grade guarantees the next grade inherits) plus full autonomy in how they teach within that framework. This is what their teachers need — a structure that supports without constraining.

Third, Mission Canvas. Governed, local-first, open source. Show the web UI. Explain: every query classified, every decision logged, PII protected, knowledge compounds with use. They own the deployment. No subscription, no dependency. The system gets smarter because teachers use it — and that intelligence stays with them.

## WHAT NOT TO SAY

Don't say "we can solve refugee education" — we can't, and it would be insulting. Don't compare their students to La Scuola students — the contexts are not comparable. Don't lead with technology — lead with pedagogy. Don't use "AI" as a magic word — explain specifically what the system does and doesn't do. Don't promise capabilities we don't have (student-facing interface, real-time assessment tracking — see the implementation brief for honest limitations).

## ANCHOR PHRASES

"We build AI systems that help teachers teach better — not by replacing them." Opening. "Zero-sum complexity — every tool must reduce burden, never add." When discussing teacher experience. "The system belongs to you. We deploy it, you own it." When they ask about dependency. "Mastery-based, not grade-based. Where you ARE, not where you should be." When discussing assessment for interrupted education. "We've built this for two schools. We want to understand if it fits yours." Humble, honest positioning.
