# Meeting Questions — June 19, 2026

**Purpose**: Understand their context deeply enough to propose specific interventions. Listen more than present.

---

## Their Current Tools and Workflow
1. What tools do teachers currently use for planning and documentation? (Toddle? Google Workspace? Paper? Custom?)
2. What does a typical teacher's planning cycle look like? (Weekly? Unit-based? Ad hoc?)
3. How do they currently share knowledge between teachers? Between campuses?

## Their Biggest Pain Points
4. What is their teachers' biggest time drain? (Lesson planning? Assessment? Reporting? Differentiation? Documentation?)
5. What breaks when a teacher leaves? What knowledge is lost?
6. What's the hardest thing about onboarding a new teacher?

## Their Students
7. How do they assess students on intake? (What's the starting point for a child who arrives mid-year with no records?)
8. How do they handle classrooms with 5+ different first languages?
9. What does "success" look like for a student after 1 year? After 3 years?

## Their IB Implementation
10. What does their IB coordinator need most? (Vertical alignment? Unit planning? Assessment moderation? Reporting?)
11. How do they adapt the IB Programme of Inquiry for students with interrupted education?
12. What's their "Revolutionary Educational Method"? (Ask respectfully — it's proprietary)

## Infrastructure
13. Internet reliability at each campus?
14. What devices are available to teachers? To students?
15. Is there IT staff at each campus, or centralized?

## Decision Making
16. Who decides whether to adopt a new tool? (Nicolò? Campus heads? Education director? Collective?)
17. Budget context — are they open to paying for AI tools, or does this need grant funding?
18. What EdTech have they tried before? What worked, what didn't, and why?

## The Italy Campus
19. What's the plan for the Italy campus? (Opening 2026 — migrant, Roma, vulnerable Italian children)
20. Will it follow the same IB framework as Nairobi/Bogotá?
21. What languages will instruction be in? (Italian? English? Both?)

*This last section matters because our Italian education expertise (Lingua Viva, La Scuola structural coherence) is directly relevant to their Italy campus.*
