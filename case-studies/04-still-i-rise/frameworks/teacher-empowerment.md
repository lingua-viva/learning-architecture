# Teacher Empowerment — Zero-Sum Complexity & Institutional Memory

**Status**: `designed` — principles validated at La Scuola, not yet applied to refugee context

---

## The Principle

Teachers at Still I Rise work in extreme conditions. Any system we propose must follow the zero-sum complexity rule:

> **Every tool must reduce teacher burden by at least as much as it costs to use.**

If we add curriculum tracking, we must eliminate an equivalent amount of manual documentation. If we add assessment rubrics, we must simplify the reporting teachers currently do. The net complexity must be zero or negative.

## Three Mechanisms

### 1. Institutional Memory (Path Records)
**Problem**: When a teacher leaves, everything they learned about their students, their methods, their curriculum adaptations leaves with them. The next teacher starts from scratch.

**Solution**: MC path records capture what was tried, what worked, and for which student profiles. Not surveillance — professional knowledge that the institution inherits. "Last year, the G3 teacher found that oral storytelling worked better than written exercises for Somali-speaking students in the first two months. Here's her documentation."

### 2. Curriculum Guidance (Ontology + Knowledge Library)
**Problem**: Teachers spend hours planning lessons that could benefit from existing knowledge — what worked at another campus, what the research says, what the IB coordinator recommends.

**Solution**: MC classifies curriculum queries through its ontology and retrieves relevant knowledge with citations. A teacher asks "How do I teach persuasive writing to students at CEFR A2?" and gets a structured response grounded in evidence, not a chatbot guess.

### 3. The 50/50 Principle (Structure + Autonomy)
**Problem**: Too much structure kills teacher creativity. Too little structure creates inconsistency between classrooms and campuses.

**Solution**: 50% standardized (non-negotiable minimums — what each grade guarantees the next grade inherits, CEFR targets, assessment rubrics) + 50% flexible (how teachers achieve those targets is their professional decision). From the La Scuola structural coherence work.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

**Monday morning, Nairobi campus:**
- Teacher opens MC, sees: "3 students showed readiness for A2 oral tasks based on last week's observations"
- MC suggests: "Two approaches that worked at Bogotá campus for similar profiles: [approach A] and [approach B]"
- Teacher chooses, adapts, teaches
- After the lesson, teacher logs a brief observation (1 minute, structured prompt)
- That observation becomes institutional knowledge — available to the next teacher, the IB coordinator, the next campus

**The teacher's burden**: 1 minute of structured logging instead of 30 minutes of freeform documentation.
**The institution's gain**: Accumulated, searchable, structured knowledge that survives turnover.

*To be validated through the Phase 1 assessment — these are design hypotheses, not proven solutions for this specific context.*
