# Assessment for Students with Interrupted Education

**Status**: `designed` — adapted from Alpha School adaptive learning architecture

---

## The Problem

Traditional assessment assumes sequential progression — each grade builds on the previous. Students with interrupted education break this assumption. A 12-year-old who missed 3 years of school is not "behind" — they have a different learning profile that includes knowledge, skills, and resilience not captured by grade-level benchmarks.

## Design Principles

1. **Start from competence, not deficit** — Assessment asks "what can you do?" before "what can't you do?" The Alpha School 6-domain profile (Academic, Social-Emotional, Language, Physical, Creative, Executive Function) provides a holistic starting point.

2. **Mastery-based progression** — Students advance when they demonstrate competence, not by calendar. No grade-level gate. A student working at G2 math and G4 oral language is not "behind in math" — they have a profile.

3. **Portfolio, not testing** — Collected evidence over time: writing samples, oral recordings, project documentation, teacher observations. Never a high-stakes single assessment.

4. **Gap detection, not gap labeling** — The system identifies "this student is ready for X next" — not "this student is missing Y." Language matters.

5. **Progressive disclosure** — Don't assess everything on day one. Build trust first. Initial assessment captures what's evident; deeper assessment emerges through relationship.

## Intake Flow (Adapted from Alpha School)

**Day 1-2**: Welcome. Name, language(s), family context. NO academic assessment.
**Day 3-5**: Informal observation. What languages does the student use? How do they interact? What interests them?
**Week 2-3**: Structured but low-stakes: oral interview in L1 (if possible) and L2. Simple literacy tasks framed as activities, not tests.
**Month 1**: First portfolio entry. Teacher narrative observation. CEFR placement (oral and literacy assessed separately).
**Ongoing**: Portfolio builds. Path records accumulate. The profile gets richer with every interaction.
