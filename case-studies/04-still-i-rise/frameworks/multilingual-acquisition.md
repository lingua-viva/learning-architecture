# CEFR Applied to Diverse L1 Populations

**Status**: `designed` — framework exists from Lingua Viva, not yet adapted for refugee context

---

## The Framework

CEFR (Common European Framework of Reference) provides language-pair agnostic proficiency levels: A1 → A2 → B1 → B2 → C1 → C2.

What Lingua Viva built for Italian L2:
- Grade-by-grade CEFR targets (A1 by end G1, A2 by end G3, B1 by end G5)
- Four components tracked: communicative functions, thematic vocabulary, oral routines, chunking
- Portfolio assessment aligned to CEFR can-do statements
- Rubrics that measure what students CAN do, not what they can't

## Adaptation Needed for Still I Rise

1. **Multiple L1s in one classroom** — Lingua Viva assumed Italian L2 with English L1. Refugee classrooms have Somali, Arabic, Swahili, French, Tigrinya L1s acquiring English or Spanish L2. The CEFR structure works for any pair, but intake assessment must identify L1 proficiency first.

2. **Non-linear progression** — Students may arrive at any point in the school year at any proficiency level. The system must support lateral entry, not just sequential progression.

3. **Oral proficiency priority** — Refugee students often develop oral fluency before literacy. Assessment must weight oral communication appropriately, not privilege written production.

4. **Code-switching as competence** — Students who switch between languages mid-sentence are demonstrating bilingual competence, not confusion. Assessment must recognize this.
