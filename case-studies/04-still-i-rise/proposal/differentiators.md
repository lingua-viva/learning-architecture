# Why Mission Canvas — Not Another EdTech

**Status**: `designed`

---

Still I Rise likely gets approached by EdTech companies regularly. What makes this different:

## 1. Governed — Every Decision Auditable
For an organization that publishes its financials and maintains 95%+ program spend, transparency matters. MC logs every query, every recommendation, every decision. The audit trail is built in, not bolted on.

## 2. Local-First — No Cloud Dependency
Most EdTech requires cloud connectivity. MC runs on local hardware. In Nairobi, Bogotá, Mumbai — where internet drops — the system keeps working. Sync when connected, work offline when not.

## 3. Teacher-Owned — Knowledge Compounds in Their System
MC doesn't extract data for a vendor's model. Every path record, every curriculum decision, every assessment insight stays in their instance. The system gets smarter because teachers use it — and that intelligence belongs to them.

## 4. Not a Product — An Owned Deployment
We deploy it. They own it. No subscription. No vendor lock-in. No "sorry, we're sunsetting this feature." The code is open source. When we step back, the system keeps working. This aligns with their anti-dependency ethos.

## 5. Built by Educators, Not Just Engineers
The frameworks come from a curriculum architect (Claudia) with 6 years of IB curriculum design and a linguist (Mical) with 12 years of knowledge architecture at Amazon. The system understands education because its designers understand education.

## 6. Proven Methodology, Honest Maturity
We don't claim to have a finished product for refugee education. We claim to have validated frameworks (structural coherence, CEFR tracking, assessment philosophy, adaptive learning) and the methodology to adapt them. The maturity labels are honest — `designed`, not `validated`. We're proposing to validate together.
