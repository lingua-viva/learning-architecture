## From Case Study 01 — Structural Coherence

**Core insight**: When multiple pedagogical traditions coexist in one institution (IB + language immersion + Reggio), they are philosophically complementary but operationally compete for limited time and resources.

**What transfers**:
- **50/50 Standardization Principle**: 50% structure (non-negotiable minimums per grade), 50% flexibility (teacher autonomy). Still I Rise teachers need clear frameworks without rigidity — their classrooms are too diverse for one-size-fits-all.
- **Vertical alignment methodology**: What each grade guarantees the next grade inherits. Critical for students who may transfer between campuses.
- **Zero-sum complexity constraint**: Adding structure → reducing equivalent burden elsewhere. Non-negotiable for overworked teachers.
- **Root cause diagnostic**: The 7-layer framework for identifying where coherence breaks. Applicable to any multi-campus institution.

**What must be adapted**: La Scuola is a single (4-campus) school in San Francisco with stable enrollment. Still I Rise has campuses on four continents with students arriving mid-year from crisis contexts.

## From Case Study 02 — AI in the Classroom

**Core insight**: AI should strengthen continuity — preserving and enriching the knowledge teachers build about students as they progress through grades.

**What transfers**:
- **Teacher observation lenses**: AI-summarized, accumulated observations that travel with the student across grades and teachers. Critical for refugee students who may have multiple teachers.
- **Tiered adaptive learning**: Universal (all students), Targeted (identified groups), Intensive (individual intervention). Maps to Still I Rise's diverse needs.
- **Knowledge accumulation**: Path records that survive teacher turnover. "Here's what we tried with students in a similar situation, here's what worked."

**What must be adapted**: The original proposal assumed stable institutional infrastructure. Still I Rise needs offline-capable, minimal-tech versions of these capabilities.

## From Case Study 03 — Lingua Viva

**Core insight**: A curriculum that integrates four frameworks (national standards + CEFR + IB + Reggio) into a single coherent manual — built collaboratively with teachers, not handed down.

**What transfers**:
- **CEFR A1→B1 vertical progression**: Measurable language acquisition targets grade by grade. Works for ANY language pair — not just Italian. Still I Rise students acquiring English in Nairobi or Spanish in Bogotá can use the same structure.
- **Four-section grade structure**: (1) disciplinary pathway, (2) CEFR progression, (3) inquiry activities, (4) portfolio assessment. This template is language-agnostic.
- **Assessment systematization**: CEFR rubrics, portfolio documentation, self-assessment, narrative observation. Exactly what's needed for students with interrupted education — measurable without being punitive.
- **Three pedagogical pillars**: Language as relationship, language as thinking, language as multimodal experience. Transferable to any language context.
- **Collaborative creation methodology**: The curriculum is designed WITH teachers, not FOR them. Teachers implement, observe, refine. The curriculum improves because the people teaching it are also designing it.

**What must be adapted**: The manual is Italian-specific. The methodology for building it — integrating multiple frameworks into vertical coherence — is the transferable asset. Still I Rise would need their own manual built using this methodology.
